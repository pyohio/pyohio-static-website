I'm an undergraduate researcher at Allegheny College, where my Junior Seminar 
project — **HueCloset**, a browser-based AI wardrobe matchmaker — led me deep 
into the intersection of color theory, image processing, and practical Python.

I built HueCloset's dominant color extraction subsystem from scratch, which is 
what this talk is all about. When I'm not clustering pixels, I'm writing about 
evaluation methodology, tuning recommendation algorithms, and figuring out why 
computers are so bad at knowing what goes with what.

This is my first conference talk.
